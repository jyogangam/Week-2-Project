# Northstar Analytics Employee Onboarding Guide

**Document ID:** OPS-005  
**Owner:** People Operations and IT Operations  
**Version:** 1.8  
**Effective date:** January 5, 2026  
**Applies to:** New United States employees and their managers

## Before the Start Date

The hiring manager submits the new-hire setup form at least five business days
before the employee's start date. IT ships the standard equipment package to
remote employees or prepares it for office pickup. The manager assigns an
onboarding partner and drafts a 30-day plan.

## First Day

New employees complete identity and payroll forms, attend orientation, enroll in
multi-factor authentication, and review required policies. Employees must not
share temporary passwords. Technical access may be limited until required
security training is complete.

## First Week

New employees should complete the security awareness course, privacy basics
course, and role-specific compliance training. Managers should review team
working hours, communication norms, and any approved remote-work arrangement.

## First 30 Days

Managers hold weekly check-ins during the first month. Employees should confirm
that system access matches their role and report unnecessary or missing access.
Employees may begin submitting normal business expenses immediately, subject to
FIN-008.

## Remote-Work Timing

New employees may receive equipment at home and may occasionally work from home
during onboarding. However, eligibility for an ongoing remote or hybrid
arrangement begins only after 90 calendar days of employment and requires the
approvals described in HR-014.

## Required Reading

New employees must review the Employee Handbook (HR-001), Remote Work Policy
(HR-014), Travel and Expense Policy (FIN-008), Information Security and Incident
Reporting Policy (SEC-003), and Data Retention and Privacy Policy (LEG-011).

## Getting Help

Questions about employment or onboarding go to People Operations. Access and
equipment issues go to IT Operations. Security concerns and suspected incidents
must be reported immediately using the process in SEC-003.

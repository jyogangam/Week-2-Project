# Northstar Analytics Employee Handbook

**Document ID:** HR-001  
**Owner:** People Operations  
**Version:** 3.2  
**Effective date:** January 15, 2026  
**Applies to:** All United States employees unless a section says otherwise

## 1. Employment and Working Hours

Northstar Analytics uses a flexible-hours model. Employees are expected to be
available for collaboration during core hours, 10:00 a.m. to 3:00 p.m. in their
local time zone, Monday through Friday. Teams may establish additional coverage
requirements when needed for customers or operational support.

Full-time employees normally work 40 hours per week. Non-exempt employees must
record all hours worked and receive manager approval before working overtime.

## 2. Paid Time Off

Full-time employees receive 20 days of paid time off (PTO) per calendar year.
Employees hired after January 1 receive a prorated amount. Up to five unused PTO
days may carry into the next calendar year. Unused days above the carryover limit
expire on December 31.

Employees should request planned PTO at least 10 business days in advance.
Managers may approve requests submitted with less notice when business coverage
allows. Sick leave and legally protected leave are administered separately from
PTO.

## 3. Holidays

Northstar observes 11 company holidays each calendar year. The annual holiday
calendar is published by People Operations each November. Employees required to
work on a company holiday should coordinate compensatory time with their manager.

For calendar year 2026, Northstar observes the following company holidays:

- New Year's Day: January 1, 2026
- Martin Luther King Jr. Day: January 19, 2026
- Presidents Day: February 16, 2026
- Memorial Day: May 25, 2026
- Juneteenth: June 19, 2026
- Independence Day observed: July 3, 2026
- Labor Day: September 7, 2026
- Thanksgiving Day: November 26, 2026
- Day after Thanksgiving: November 27, 2026
- Winter Holiday: December 24, 2026
- Christmas Day: December 25, 2026

## 4. Remote and Hybrid Work

Remote and hybrid work arrangements are governed by the Remote Work Policy
(HR-014). The Remote Work Policy defines eligibility, office attendance,
equipment, and working-location requirements. Nothing in this handbook overrides
HR-014.

## 5. Business Travel and Expenses

Employees must follow the Travel and Expense Policy (FIN-008) when purchasing
travel or requesting reimbursement. Employees should not assume that manager
approval makes an otherwise prohibited expense reimbursable.

## 6. Standards of Conduct

Employees must protect confidential company, customer, and employee information.
Suspected loss or unauthorized disclosure of information must be reported using
the process in the Information Security and Incident Reporting Policy (SEC-003).

## 7. Questions and Exceptions

People Operations may interpret this handbook and approve written exceptions to
HR-owned provisions. Exceptions involving finance, security, or legal
requirements must also be approved by the owner of the applicable policy.

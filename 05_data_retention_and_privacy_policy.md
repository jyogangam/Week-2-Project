# Northstar Analytics Data Retention and Privacy Policy

**Document ID:** LEG-011  
**Owner:** Legal and Privacy  
**Version:** 2.4  
**Effective date:** May 1, 2026  
**Applies to:** All Northstar business records and personal data

## 1. Core Principles

Northstar collects and retains only data needed for a documented business or
legal purpose. Personal data must be accurate, access-controlled, and deleted or
anonymized when its approved retention period ends, unless a legal hold applies.

## 2. Standard Retention Periods

- Customer contracts: seven years after contract termination.
- Vendor contracts: seven years after contract termination.
- Employee personnel files: seven years after employment ends.
- Unsuccessful candidate records: two years after the hiring decision.
- Routine system logs: 90 days.
- Security investigation records: three years after the investigation closes.
- Expense reports and supporting receipts: seven years after the end of the
  applicable fiscal year.

## 3. Legal Holds

A legal hold suspends normal deletion for identified records. When Legal issues a
hold notice, recipients must preserve the listed information until Legal sends a
written release. A retention period expiring does not override an active legal
hold.

## 4. Privacy Requests

Requests from individuals to access, correct, or delete personal data must be
sent to the Privacy team through the Privacy Request form. Employees must not
promise deletion or provide records directly unless authorized by Privacy.

Privacy verifies the requester's identity, determines which laws apply, and
coordinates the response. Northstar aims to acknowledge requests within five
business days, but the final response deadline depends on applicable law.

## 5. Security Incidents Involving Personal Data

Suspected loss, unauthorized disclosure, or misuse of personal data must be
reported immediately under SEC-003. Information Security leads the initial
incident response and involves Legal and Privacy as needed.

## 6. Approved Disposal

Paper records containing Confidential or Restricted information must be placed in
locked shredding bins. Electronic records must be deleted using approved system
processes. Moving records to a personal account or local drive is not an approved
preservation method.

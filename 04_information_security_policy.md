# Northstar Analytics Information Security and Incident Reporting Policy

**Document ID:** SEC-003  
**Owner:** Information Security  
**Version:** 5.3  
**Effective date:** April 10, 2026  
**Applies to:** Employees, contractors, temporary workers, and vendors with access
to Northstar systems or information

## 1. Data Classification

Northstar information is classified as Public, Internal, Confidential, or
Restricted.

- **Public:** Approved for public release.
- **Internal:** Routine business information intended only for Northstar workers.
- **Confidential:** Sensitive company, customer, partner, or employee information.
- **Restricted:** Highly sensitive information requiring the strongest controls,
  including passwords, authentication secrets, government identification
  numbers, payment-card data, and unencrypted production customer data.

## 2. Access and Authentication

Workers must use unique company accounts and multi-factor authentication where
available. Passwords and authentication codes must never be shared. Access must
be limited to what is required for the worker's role.

## 3. Approved Storage and Sharing

Confidential and Restricted data must be stored only in approved company systems.
Restricted data must not be copied to personal devices, personal email, or
consumer file-sharing services. Confidential data may be shared externally only
for an approved business purpose and with appropriate contractual protections.

## 4. Generative AI Tools

Public information may be entered into any approved generative AI tool.
Confidential information may be entered only into the Northstar-managed AI
environment. Restricted information must not be entered into any generative AI
tool, including the Northstar-managed environment.

## 5. Incident Reporting

Workers must immediately report suspected security incidents through the Security
Hotline in the employee portal. If the portal is unavailable, call the 24-hour
Security Operations number printed on the company badge.

Examples include a lost or stolen company device, suspected phishing, malware,
misdirected email containing Confidential or Restricted data, exposed
credentials, or unauthorized system access. Do not delay reporting while trying
to investigate or fix the issue yourself.

## 6. Lost or Stolen Devices

Immediately contact the Security Hotline and inform your manager. Security will
decide whether remote lock or wipe actions are required. For a device lost during
business travel, also notify the travel provider or local authorities when
appropriate, but do not postpone the Security Hotline report.

## 7. Policy Exceptions

Information Security must approve exceptions in writing. Exceptions involving
Restricted data also require Legal approval.
